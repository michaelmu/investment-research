#### AAON (AAON) — AI data-center cooling backlog as a 2nd‑order winner

**Category:** Pickaxe / enabler (physical infrastructure for AI data centers)

**Why it benefits from AI progress (mechanism):** AI training + inference pushes data-center rack densities higher, which raises the value of specialized thermal management (air + liquid). AAON’s BASX segment sells purpose-built cooling equipment (including liquid cooling interfaces like CDUs) into hyperscale/colocation buildouts; as AI/HPC capex grows, cooling becomes a gating item (time-to-build + power/thermal constraints), pulling through higher-value engineered systems and services.

##### 10‑K grounding (primary-source facts)
Filing: **Form 10‑K**, **filingDate 2026-03-02**, **reportDate 2025-12-31**, **accession 0000824142-26-000005**.

- **BASX is explicitly positioned for AI/HPC workloads.** The Business section notes BASX data center cooling solutions are designed to support “**AI and high-performance computing (HPC)** workloads,” including a proprietary **Coolant Distribution Unit (CDU)** introduced in 2025 that “supports rack densities **exceeding 100 kilowatts**” with redundancy and leak detection features (Item 1).
