#### Equinix (EQIX) — interconnection flywheel + AI-driven demand, wrapped in a REIT

**SEC 10‑K grounding (primary source):** Accession **0001101239-26-000032**; **filing date 2026-02-11**; **report date 2025-12-31**.

Equinix is the “digital infrastructure” tollbooth for getting compute, networks, and partners close together. The core idea is a **network effect**: once you have the densest set of customers in the right metros, everyone else wants to be in the same buildings because direct connections are cheaper/faster and more reliable than going out over the public internet.

Two scale facts from the latest 10‑K show why that flywheel is hard to replicate. First, Equinix reports a footprint of **280 data centers across 77 markets in 36 countries** (Item 1, *Business*). Second, it says it serves **over 10,500 customers** and has curated **more than 500,000 interconnections** (Item 1). That customer density is the moat: adding one more cloud/network/enterprise tends to increase the value of the campus for everyone already there.

**Where the growth can come from:** (1) continued “hybrid” enterprise architecture (some on-prem, some cloud, lots of partners), which increases the number of private connections; and (2) AI buildouts, which increase demand for high-density capacity and low-latency connectivity across clouds and data sources. Equinix explicitly frames AI as increasing the need for distributed, interconnected infrastructure (Item 1).

**What can break the thesis:** supply chain and build costs. The risk section flags that **U.S.–China tensions and decoupling efforts can raise supply-chain risk and tariffs**, with Equinix noting potential impacts from tariffs (and specifically calling out steel and steel derivatives as a potential cost driver) (Item 1A, *Risk Factors*). This matters because the business must keep building and expanding to satisfy demand and defend its ecosystem density.

**China exposure:** No direct revenue concentration in China was apparent from the excerpts reviewed; however, the 10‑K explicitly highlights **China-related supply chain/tariff risk** (Item 1A). Treat as **low/unknown** and monitor.
