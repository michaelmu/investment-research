#### Comfort Systems USA (FIX) — “picks-and-shovels” for AI data-center buildouts

**Company / ticker / category:** Comfort Systems USA, Inc. (NYSE: **FIX**) — **pickaxe/enabler** (MEP contractor enabling data centers and high-density compute facilities).

##### AI tailwind thesis (mechanism)
AI progress drives *physical* buildout: more data centers, higher power density, more heat rejection, faster schedules, and tighter uptime requirements. FIX benefits as a scaled U.S. contractor that designs/installs/maintains the **mechanical, electrical, and plumbing (MEP)** backbone of those facilities—HVAC/cooling, electrical systems, controls, piping, and increasingly **off-site / modular** fabrication that shortens construction timelines. As AI training/inference spreads, the “compute box” spend should pull through to sustained demand for MEP installation plus recurring service/retrofit work.

##### Grounded 10‑K facts (primary source)
- FIX operates **50 operating units with 190 locations in 142 cities throughout the United States** (Item 1 — *Business*), which helps it serve geographically distributed hyperscale/enterprise builds with local crews.
- **Technology was 45.0% of 2025 revenue** by end-use sector (Item 1 — *Business*), indicating meaningful current exposure to the same customer budget category that AI capex expands.
- As of **Dec 31, 2025**, FIX had **8,427 projects in process** with an **aggregate contract value of ~$24.17B**; average project size was **~$2.9M** and typical duration **6–9 months** (Item 1 — *Business*).
- Reported **backlog of $11.94B** as of Dec 31, 2025, but notes backlog can be adjusted/canceled and may not translate to profits (Item 1A — *Risk Factors*).

##### Price/trend context (10y)
10-year trend is very strong: **~+5,279% total return** and **~49% CAGR** (price-history summary through 2026‑02‑24). Shares are near highs (fromHigh ≈ **-1.7%**), and above long-term trend (aboveSMA200 ≈ **+78.6%**)—great momentum, but valuation/mean-reversion risk is real.

##### KPIs to watch
- **Backlog** and conversion (revenue growth vs. backlog growth)
- **Technology end-market mix** and customer concentration (one customer was **12.8% of 2025 revenue**; Item 1A)
- **Project margins** (cost overruns / labor & materials inflation)

##### Main risks
- **Cyclicality + customer capex timing:** construction downturns or AI capex pauses (Item 1A).
- **Execution risk:** FIX bears cost-overrun risk on most contracts; schedule/performance guarantees can create damages (Item 1A).
- **Customer concentration:** loss/slowdown of a major customer (Item 1A).
- **China exposure:** operations are described as U.S.-based (Item 1), but supply chain includes “domestic or foreign suppliers” for components (Item 1). **China-specific dependency is not clearly quantified here → label as *unknown* risk**.
