#### W.W. Grainger (GWW) — "MRO distributor compounding on AI-driven capex + AI-native procurement"

**Category:** adopter (AI-enabled distribution + procurement workflow)

**Filing context:** Form 10‑K filingDate **2026‑02‑19**; reportDate **2025‑12‑31**; accession **0000277135-26-000011**.

##### AI tailwind thesis (2nd/3rd-order)
The biggest equity winners from AI aren’t only the chip/compute layer—there’s a large “real-economy plumbing” layer. As AI drives **data center buildouts**, grid upgrades, and broader industrial retooling, the day‑2 operating burden rises: more equipment, more uptime requirements, more parts, and more procurement complexity. **Grainger** is positioned to compound through that complexity by (1) being the dependable MRO source of supply for large customers and (2) making buying frictionless via eCommerce + AI features (search, discovery, recommendations, service automation).

##### Grounded facts from the 10‑K
- Grainger operates a **bifurcated model** with two reportable segments: **High‑Touch Solutions N.A.** and **Endless Assortment** (Zoro + MonotaRO) (Item 1).
- The **Endless Assortment** segment includes **Zoro (U.S.)** and **MonotaRO (Japan)**, and these platforms emphasize “intelligent analytic capabilities” in their eCommerce experience (Item 1).
- Grainger explicitly states it has increased and expects to continue increasing investments in **AI (including large language model technologies)**, aiming to incorporate AI into its digital platforms and internal operations (Item 1A).
- FY2025 net sales were **$17.942B**; High‑Touch Solutions N.A. **$13.993B** and Endless Assortment **$3.625B** (Item 7, MD&A).

##### Key KPIs to track
- **Endless Assortment growth** (FY2025 +15.7% reported) and operating earnings leverage (Item 7).
- **Digital penetration / traffic acquisition efficiency** (ad spend productivity; risk if search/ads shift) (Item 1A).
- **KeepStock / inventory management adoption** (sticky workflow + switching costs) (Item 1).

##### Risks (incl. China exposure constraint)
- **AI execution risk:** Grainger warns AI may not deliver customer‑preferred solutions and may introduce regulatory, IP, and reputational risks (Item 1A).
- **Platform dependence:** reliance on search engines/digital advertising rules can raise CAC or reduce traffic (Item 1A).
- **China exposure:** the 10‑K text scan did not surface explicit “China/PRC” references; however, **product sourcing/supply chain exposure is not fully knowable from this excerpt** → treat as **unknown risk** pending deeper supplier country-of-origin disclosure review.
