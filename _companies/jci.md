#### Johnson Controls (JCI) — focused building-tech + service backlog flywheel

**SEC 10‑K grounding:** Johnson Controls International plc (JCI) Form 10‑K **filed 2025‑11‑14** for fiscal year ended **2025‑09‑30** (accession **0000833444-25-000097**).

Johnson Controls is a building-technology roll-up that has spent the last decade consolidating HVAC, fire, security, controls and related services (Item 1, *Business*). What’s interesting for a “compounder” lens is the combination of (1) a huge installed base that drives recurring service/retrofit work and (2) increasingly software-enabled workflows (OpenBlue) that can improve attachment and retention.

Two concrete 10‑K facts that frame the current shape of the business:

- **Portfolio simplification:** JCI completed the **divestiture of its Residential and Light Commercial (R&LC) HVAC business on July 31, 2025** (Item 1, *Business – General*). Management explicitly describes a move to a “simpler, more focused company” after divesting Air Distribution Technologies and R&LC HVAC (Item 1, *Business Strategy*). This matters because residential HVAC is typically more cyclical/competitive than commercial, and the divestiture may increase mix toward higher-value solutions and services.
- **Recurrence + visibility:** In fiscal 2025, **products and systems were ~68% of sales from continuing operations and services were ~32%** (Item 1, *Products, Systems, Services and Solutions*). Additionally, JCI reported **$16.6B of backlog at September 30, 2025** (Item 1, *Backlog and Orders*). A meaningful service component plus backlog can support steadier compounding if execution remains tight.

**China exposure / geopolitics (constraint check):** JCI’s 10‑K explicitly notes that fiscal 2024–2025 results were impacted by **softening economic conditions in China**, which **negatively impacted APAC** (Item 1A, *Risk Factors*). That’s a direct signal that China is *economically meaningful* to results via the APAC segment; treat this as a real risk given the mandate to avoid meaningful China exposure.

**10‑year price context (trend only):** Over the last 10 years, JCI shows ~**15.9% CAGR** with a **max drawdown ~‑47%**; as of 2026‑03‑06 it’s ~**9% below** its 10‑year high and above both the 50‑ and 200‑day moving averages (price_history.py; unadjusted).

**My take:** If you want “electrification/data center adjacent” exposure through HVAC/controls + services, JCI is credible—*but* the explicit China sensitivity means it’s not a clean fit for a strict “avoid China” mandate. I’d only underwrite it with a clear view of APAC/China contribution and a belief that services/software attachment can offset cyclicality.
