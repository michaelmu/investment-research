#### MSCI (MSCI) — index + analytics tollbooth compounder

##### Thesis
MSCI is a “picks-and-shovels” compounder for global capital markets: it sells mission‑critical indexes, analytics, and data that plug directly into client workflows (subscriptions + asset‑based fees). The product becomes embedded (benchmarks, ETF licensing, risk models, regulatory reporting), creating high switching costs and durable pricing power. If passive/indexed assets, multi‑asset risk management, and regulatory/sustainability reporting keep growing, MSCI can compound revenue and free cash flow with relatively modest incremental capital.

##### 10-K grounded facts (2+; cite Item/section)
- **Large, recurring index franchise with meaningful asset‑based upside/volatility.** For FY2025, **57.0% of revenues** were attributable to the **Index** segment, and **asset‑based fees were 43.1% of Index revenues** (Item 1, “Segments — Index”). This is a strong toll‑booth model, but it ties a slice of revenue to market levels and AUM.
- **Customer concentration is real.** As of Dec 31, 2025, **BlackRock accounted for 10.8% of consolidated operating revenues**, and **96.5%** of revenue from BlackRock came from fees based on assets in BlackRock index‑based products (Item 1, “Clients”).
- **Strong cash generation (FCF proxy).** Net cash provided by operating activities was **$1.588B** in 2025, with **capex $39.3M** and **capitalized software $90.5M** (Item 8, Consolidated Statements of Cash Flows). A simple FCF proxy (OCF – capex – capitalized software) is **~$1.46B**.

Filing: Form 10‑K; **filingDate 2026‑02‑06**, **reportDate 2025‑12‑31**, **accession 0001408198-26-000011**.

##### Risks / disconfirmers
- **Market sensitivity:** asset‑based fees can fall with equity drawdowns and AUM shifts (see Index revenue mix in Item 1).
- **Key‑client pressure:** large clients can negotiate price, self‑index, or shift to competitors; concentration (BlackRock 10.8%) raises bargaining‑power risk (Item 1).
- **Regulatory / reputational risk:** MSCI operates in regulated benchmark/ratings-adjacent domains; errors, methodology disputes, and changing ESG/climate rules can drive scrutiny and churn (Risk Factors summary in Item 1A).
- **Platform / data dependency and cyber:** reliance on third‑party data sources and the need to protect confidential data + uptime are recurring risks (Item 1A summary).

##### China exposure check
**Likely non-zero; magnitude unknown.** The 10‑K states **MSCI Limited is registered with the Cyberspace Administration of China** as a foreign institution supplying financial information services in China (Item 1, “Regulation”). Revenue mix by geography attributable to China was **not identified in the extracted sections**, so treat as a **risk/unknown**.
