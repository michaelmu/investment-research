#### Verisk (VRSK): an embedded “tollbooth” on U.S. insurance workflows

**Source 10-K (SEC):** Verisk Analytics, Inc. Form 10‑K, **filingDate 2025‑02‑26**, **reportDate 2024‑12‑31**, accession **0001437749-25-005160**.

Verisk is a classic insurance-ecosystem compounder: it owns data assets and embeds analytics + compliance content directly into carrier workflows (rating, underwriting, claims, catastrophe, regulatory reporting). That “workflow gravity” matters because insurers don’t casually swap out tools that are tied to filings, pricing, and loss-cost assumptions.

Two grounding facts from the 10‑K:

- **Recurring revenue mix:** Verisk states it sells solutions “primarily through annual subscriptions or long-term agreements… typically prepaid,” and that these subscription arrangements were **~81% of 2024 revenue** (Item 1, *Insurance Segment*).
- **Scale/data moat:** In 2024, P&C insurers sent Verisk **~2.7 billion individual transaction records**, and Verisk reports maintaining an underwriting database of **~36.3 billion statistical records** (Item 1, *Insurance Segment / statistical agent services*). This scale is hard to replicate and feeds model performance + product breadth.

Business quality signals: Verisk says its 2024 clients included **all of the top 100 U.S. P&C insurance providers** for the lines it serves (Item 1, *Our Company*). It also highlights heavy regulatory “plumbing” (e.g., processing **~2,000 regulatory filings** annually and interfacing with regulators across all 50 states + territories) which reinforces switching costs (Item 1, *Forms, Rules, and Loss Costs*).

**China exposure (constraint check):** The 10‑K explicitly mentions serving clients in **China** and having offices there, and notes certain catastrophe/crop models covering China (Item 1). I’d treat the magnitude of revenue exposure as **unknown** from this excerpt and flag it as a diligence item.

**10‑year price context:** Over the last decade VRSK’s price CAGR is about **9.3%** (per price_history summary). Shares are roughly **~44% below** their prior high, which suggests multiple compression or growth concerns rather than a simple “straight line” compounder—worth underwriting whether insurance analytics demand and pricing power remain intact.

**Watchlist action:** **ADD** — sticky subscription-like model + proprietary data scale; diligence the *actual* non‑U.S./China revenue share and any regulatory/competitive pressure in core ISO-style products.
