### Data-center load growth as a scarcity amplifier

#### VST — Data-center load growth as a scarcity amplifier (nuclear PPAs + uprates)

##### AI tailwind thesis (mechanism)
AI progress drives *electricity demand growth* (hyperscale data centers + related grid investment). Vistra is positioned as a second/third‑order AI winner because (1) it owns dispatchable generation (notably nuclear + gas) in key competitive markets, and (2) it can monetize incremental load via long-dated offtake/PPAs with creditworthy counterparties, potentially converting volatile merchant exposure into more durable cash flows. If AI‑driven load tightens reserve margins, scarcity pricing and higher capacity values can improve realized economics for existing dispatchable fleets—especially nuclear, where variable costs are low and output is steady.

##### 10-K grounded facts (2+; cite Item/section)
- Vistra describes itself as an integrated retail electricity + power generation company operating in **18 states + DC**, serving **~5 million** retail customers, with a generation fleet of **~44,000 MW** across **natural gas (62%) / coal (20%) / nuclear (15%) / solar+battery (3%)** (Item 1, *Business – General / Electricity Generation Operations*).
- Management highlights signing long-term nuclear PPAs with hyperscalers: a **20‑year PPA with AWS for 1,200 MW** from Comanche Peak (delivery expected to begin **4Q27** and ramp to full by **2032**), and **20‑year PPAs with Meta totaling 2,609 MW** from PJM nuclear plants (including **433 MW** of planned uprates; operating deliveries expected late **2026–2027**, uprate deliveries **2031–2034**) (Item 7, *MD&A – Key Financial Results / Noteworthy Developments*).
- The risk section explicitly ties load growth to AI and data centers: it cites “large load data centers…including in response to…artificial intelligence (AI)” as a demand driver and warns that slower AI adoption or more energy‑efficient AI could reduce expected demand growth and hurt performance (Item 1A, *Risk Factors – Electricity demand / large load offtake opportunities*).
