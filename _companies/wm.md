#### Waste Management (WM) — AI-enabled routing + automation as margin flywheel

**Company / ticker:** Waste Management, Inc. (**WM**)  
**Category:** **Adopter** (operational AI/automation; not an AI “pickaxe” vendor)

##### AI tailwind thesis (mechanism)
As AI-driven optimization, sensing, and machine-vision improve, WM can translate “better decisions per route / per facility / per ton” into (1) lower labor + fuel cost-to-serve, (2) higher asset utilization (trucks, transfer, landfill), and (3) better commodity recovery economics in recycling. This is a **2nd-order AI winner**: AI progress reduces the cost and increases the reliability of applying optimization/vision to physical operations, which widens the gap between scaled incumbents with dense networks and smaller operators.

##### Grounding from the 10-K (primary source)
**10-K:** filingDate **2026-02-09**, reportDate **2025-12-31**, accession **0001104659-26-012049**.

- **Scale + network density:** WM states it **“own[s] or operate[s] 257 landfill sites”** and **“manage[s] 342 transfer stations”** in the U.S. and Canada (Item **1**, Business). Network density is what makes route optimization and asset scheduling compounding advantages.
- **Recycling footprint + automation leverage:** As of **Dec 31, 2025**, WM operated **113 recycling facilities**, including **51 single-stream** facilities (Item **1**, Business). Machine-vision + improved sorting/quality control are direct beneficiaries of AI progress.
- **Customer concentration check:** WM discloses its **largest customer represented <5% of annual revenues** (Item **1**, Business), reducing single-customer risk as WM invests into tech-driven process changes.
- **AI explicitly flagged as a strategic risk/necessity:** WM warns that **“inability to adapt to and manage the benefits and risks of artificial intelligence could expose us to liability or put us at a disadvantage”** (Item **1A**, Risk Factors). That’s a useful signal that AI is becoming relevant even in “non-tech” industrial services.

##### Key KPIs to track
- Collection **cost-to-serve** (labor hours/stop, fuel per route), safety incidents
- **Recycling economics:** contamination rates, recovered commodity yield/value per ton
- **Landfill/transfer utilization** and pricing (yield + retention)
- Capex intensity and returns on automation initiatives

##### Risks / constraints
- **China exposure:** appears **limited operationally** (business is U.S./Canada-heavy), but WM notes it **markets/resells recycling commodities globally** (Item 1). Treat end-market/regulatory exposure as **unknown** without a segment-level revenue breakout by country.
- Tech execution risk: automation/AI projects can under-deliver; labor relations and regulatory constraints can slow change.
- Commodity price volatility impacts recycling profitability.
