## Veeva Systems (VEEV)

# Veeva Systems (VEEV) — vertical SaaS compounder in life sciences

**SEC 10-K grounding:** Veeva Systems Inc. Form 10‑K (accession **0001393052-25-000022**), **filed 2025‑03‑24**, for fiscal year ended **2025‑01‑31** (report date 2025‑01‑31).

Veeva is a classic “vertical SaaS” compounder: it sells workflow software, data products, and services purpose-built for the life sciences industry, spanning R&D through commercialization (Item 1, *Business*). What makes this interesting is the combination of (1) mission-critical, regulated workflows (clinical, regulatory, quality) and (2) large, entrenched customers with long product cycles—conditions that tend to create durable switching costs and high retention.

**Concrete 10‑K facts that matter:**
- Veeva explicitly positions itself as “the leading provider of industry cloud solutions for the global life sciences industry,” with offerings spanning **cloud software, data, and business consulting** (Item 1, *Overview*).
- As of **January 31, 2025**, Veeva served **1,477 customers** and had **7,291 employees worldwide** (Item 1, *Our Customers*; *Human Capital Resources*).

**Why it can compound:** Veeva groups products into Development Cloud, Quality Cloud, Commercial Cloud, and Data Cloud (Item 1). That product map creates natural cross-sell paths: a customer that starts in one function (e.g., clinical) can expand into adjacent regulated workflows (regulatory/safety/quality) and later into commercial execution and data subscriptions. Importantly, Veeva highlights an ability to “quickly adapt to regulatory changes” and ship relevant enhancements rapidly (Item 1, *Overview*), which is a real moat in regulated software: compliance drift is costly for customers.

**Key risk / underwriting questions:**
- **Platform dependency / product transition:** Veeva notes that **Veeva CRM** and some related applications are built on a platform provided by **Salesforce** and “will be supported until **September 1, 2030**” (Item 1, *Veeva Commercial Cloud*). The long runway helps, but the migration path to Vault CRM still matters for competitive positioning and customer friction.
- **China exposure:** Veeva is global; the 10‑K references geographic regions (including Asia Pacific) in its financial statement notes/table of contents. I did not quantify PRC-specific revenue from the extracted sections here, so treat **China exposure as *unknown* (risk)** until we pull Note 16 / geographic revenue detail.

**10‑year price context (trend only):** Over the last ~10 years, VEEV shows a strong long-run uptrend with ~**21.9% CAGR** and ~**+626% total return**; shares are currently about **47% below** the 10‑year high (last close **$182.01** vs high **$343.96**), and below the 50/200‑day averages (price history tool, stooq).

**WATCHLIST action:** **ADD** — regulated vertical SaaS with broad suite + expansion vectors; diligence next: quantify PRC/China revenue exposure and validate CRM platform-transition execution.
