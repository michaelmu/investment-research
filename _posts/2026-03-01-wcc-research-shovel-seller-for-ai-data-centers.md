# WESCO International (WCC) — “Shovel seller” for AI data centers

**Category:** enabler (distribution + lifecycle services)

**Why it benefits from AI progress (2nd/3rd-order):** As AI model training/inference drives **more data center builds and retrofits**, the bottleneck shifts toward *power distribution, cabling/connectivity, cooling-supporting electrical gear, and on-the-ground deployment + maintenance*. WESCO sits in the spend path as a scaled distributor + solutions integrator (products + services) across the **data center lifecycle** (design/development through operations), so AI-driven capex translates into higher volumes, backlog, and services attach.

**Primary-source anchors (10-K):**
- **CSS is explicitly a “global leader” in data center solutions** and its portfolio targets “**hyperscale data centers**” among other environments (Item 1, *Business*).
- Management states secular growth exposure to “**AI-driven data centers**,” and in FY2025 notes that the **CSS data center business is primarily driving growth** (Item 7, *MD&A*).
- Segment detail: **CSS net sales were $9.1B in 2025 vs $7.7B in 2024 (+18.3%)**, with **organic sales up 16.7% driven primarily by volume growth… as a result of the data center solutions business** (Item 7, *MD&A*).
- Capability expansion: WESCO acquired **Ascent (data center facility management services; 300+ employees in the U.S. & Canada)** and **entroCIM (data center/building intelligence software)** to extend data center lifecycle offerings (Item 8, Note on acquisitions).

**Filing metadata:** 10-K filed **2026-02-13** for period ending **2025-12-31** (report date **2025-12-31**); accession **0000929008-26-000008**.

**10y price context (trend only):** ~**20.6% CAGR** over 10y; last close **$289.5** (2026-02-27), about **-9.4%** from 10y high; trading **~25% above 200d SMA** (momentum still positive).

**Key KPIs to track:**
- CSS segment: net sales growth, organic volume vs price mix, adjusted EBITDA / margin.
- Backlog growth (esp. CSS + UBS), project mix (large hyperscale projects can pressure gross margin).
- Services attach/recurring mix (facility management + software) vs pure product distribution.

**Risks (incl. China constraint):**
- **Project/mix risk:** large project sales can dilute gross margin (management flags this in MD&A).
- **Competitive/price pressure** in distribution + integrator channels.
- **AI/cyber/third-party platform dependence**: the 10-K adds a detailed AI risk factor (Item 1A).
- **China exposure:** the extracted 10-K text scan showed **no explicit China/PRC mention**, but supply-chain origin for electrical/components is **unknown** from this quick pass → treat as a diligence item.
