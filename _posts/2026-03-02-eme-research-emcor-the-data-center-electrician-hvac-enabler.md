## EMCOR Group (EME)

# EMCOR Group (EME) — the “data center electrician/HVAC” enabler hiding in plain sight

**Category:** Enabler (specialty contracting + facilities services that make AI data centers and high‑tech facilities physically work)

**Why it’s an AI beneficiary (2nd/3rd‑order):**
AI buildouts translate into *real-world* demand for (a) dense electrical distribution, (b) backup/UPS, (c) mechanical/HVAC + environmental controls, (d) low‑voltage/fiber cabling, and (e) ongoing maintenance/retrofits. EME is positioned to capture that spend because it sits in the “last mile” of power/cooling/controls inside the facility—where complexity and uptime requirements rise as AI racks get hotter and power-hungrier.

## 10‑K grounding (primary source)
Filing: **10‑K** | **filingDate:** 2026-02-26 | **reportDate:** 2025-12-31 | **accession:** 0000105634-26-000025.

Concrete facts:
- **Scale + US concentration:** EMCOR reports **$16.99B** of 2025 revenue and notes **~97%** of 2025 revenues were generated in the **United States** (Item 1, *Business – Overview*). That helps the “avoid China exposure” constraint; **China revenue is not discussed** in the extracted text (treat as **unknown** risk until explicitly confirmed).
- **AI/data center explicit demand driver:** The 10‑K states industry growth is driven in part by “**cloud computing… and the emergence of artificial intelligence (AI)**,” and that recent demand has benefited from “**the expansion of data centers to power AI and cloud computing**” (Item 1, *Business – Operations*).
- **Backlog / forward demand signal:** Remaining performance obligations were **$13.25B** at 12/31/2025 (up from $10.10B) with the “most significant increases” in **network & communications**, “predominantly as a result of several **data center construction contracts**” (Item 7, *MD&A*).

## Key KPIs to track
- **Remaining performance obligations / backlog** (total + mix to network/communications).
- **Segment margins** (especially US electrical + mechanical construction) and evidence of execution quality.
- **Labor availability/productivity** (union/field labor is a binding constraint).

## Risks / watch‑outs
- **Cyclicality of AI capex:** EMCOR warns that if rapidly expanding data center infrastructure spending slows, demand could decline (Item 1A, *Risk Factors*).
- **Fixed-price contract risk:** profit depends on cost estimates; inflation/labor/material swings can compress margins (Item 1A).
- **Power availability constraint:** 10‑K notes electricity availability/price can impact data center buildouts (Item 1A).
- **China exposure:** not clearly quantified here; treat supply-chain dependency as **unknown** until we find explicit disclosure.
