## HEICO Corporation (HEI)

# HEICO (HEI) — FAA-approved aerospace aftermarket + “bolt-on” acquirer

**Why this might be a compounder:** HEICO is a niche aerospace/defense manufacturer with a long runway in **FAA-approved replacement parts** (priced below OEM alternatives) plus a repeatable, disciplined acquisition engine.

## Grounding in the latest 10-K (primary source)
**SEC 10-K metadata:** filed **2025-12-22** for period ended **2025-10-31** (reportDate 2025-10-31), accession **0000046619-25-000082**.

- **Segment mix / business model:** HEICO reports two segments: the **Flight Support Group (FSG)** and **Electronic Technologies Group (ETG)**. FSG represented **70% of net sales in fiscal 2025** (ETG **30%**) and sells FAA-approved replacement parts, repair/overhaul, distribution, and military support services. (10-K **Item 1 — Business**)
- **Growth + profitability snapshot:** FY2025 net sales were **$4,485.0M**, up **16%** YoY, with **gross margin 39.8%** and **operating margin 22.7%**. Management attributes FSG’s growth to **14% organic** plus acquisition contribution, and ETG to **7% organic** plus acquisition contribution. (10-K **Item 7 — MD&A**, “Comparison of Fiscal 2025 to Fiscal 2024”)

A notable structural advantage is regulatory/engineering know-how: management says they are adding **~400–550 FAA Parts Manufacturer Approvals (PMAs) per year** and have developed **~20,000** FAA-approved parts over time—an embedded barrier to entry in this niche. (10-K **Item 1 — Business**)

## What the market exposure looks like (and what to watch)
HEICO’s FY2025 sales mix by end-market was **~58% commercial aviation**, **~31% defense/space**, and **~11% other industrial** (electronics/medical/telecom). (10-K **Item 7 — MD&A**)

**China exposure:** I did not see “China/PRC” referenced in the extracted 10-K text; revenue could still include indirect exposure via distributors or end-customers. Treat as **unknown** until quantified from segment/geography disclosures.

## 10-year price context (trend only)
Over the last ~10 years (Stooq, unadjusted OHLC), HEI shows ~**27.1% CAGR** and ~**-57.7% max drawdown**, with the latest close **$329.74**, about **-8.8%** off the 10y high. This is consistent with “quality compounder” behavior punctuated by cyclical drawdowns.

## One-sentence thesis
A long-duration compounder candidate driven by (1) a growing catalog of FAA-approved aftermarket parts + repair/overhaul pull-through and (2) a proven, disciplined acquisition flywheel—tempered by aerospace cyclicality, defense budget cycles, and any unquantified foreign-market exposure.
