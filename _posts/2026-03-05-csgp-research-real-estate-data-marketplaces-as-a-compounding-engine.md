## CoStar Group (CSGP)

# CoStar Group (CSGP) — real estate data + marketplaces as a compounding engine

**Context (SEC grounding):** This note is based on CoStar Group’s latest Form 10‑K (accession **0001057352-26-000020**; filingDate **2026‑02‑26**; reportDate **2025‑12‑31**).

CoStar is essentially trying to be the “system of record” for real estate: it runs large subscription information products (CoStar, STR, lease-management SaaS like Visual Lease/CoStar Real Estate Manager) and consumer-facing marketplaces (Apartments.com, Homes.com, LoopNet, etc.). The compounding setup is straightforward: proprietary data + workflow tools + marketplaces reinforce each other, and the product suite creates real cross‑sell pathways.

**What’s durable here (moat mechanics):** In **Item 1 (Business)** the company emphasizes a standardized platform built around a proprietary commercial real estate database and a large in‑house research organization (“the largest research department in the commercial real estate industry”). That matters because data in CRE is messy; CoStar’s approach combines continuous human collection/verification with automation/AI to improve research efficiency and data quality (Item 1). The second moat layer is distribution: most services are delivered under **subscription-based license agreements that typically renew automatically**, with “a majority” having terms of at least **one year** and priced as fixed monthly subscriptions rather than click/usage-based fees (Item 1). That structure is exactly what you want for a high-quality compounder: recurring revenue with contractual stickiness tied to workflow.

**Recent strategic shape-shifting (and risk):** CoStar has been leaning into inorganic growth. The 10‑K states it acquired **Visual Lease (Nov 2024), Matterport (Feb 2025), and Domain (Aug 2025)** and is integrating those offerings into the network (Item 1). That can widen the product surface area (especially Matterport’s “3D digital twin” angle), but it also raises execution risk: integration, capital allocation discipline, and avoiding “too many fronts” while Homes.com/consumer brands demand heavy marketing.

**Price context (10y trend, unadjusted):** Over the last ~10 years, CSGP shows roughly **~10% CAGR** with a **~55% max drawdown** and the stock currently ~**52%** below its 10‑year high (last close **$48.41** vs high **$101.05**; stooq; unadjusted). That’s not a timing signal, but it’s a reminder that even great data businesses can be volatile when ad/transactional expectations or spend cycles shift.

**China exposure check:** The Business section lists meaningful operations/sales presence across the U.S., Canada, the U.K., parts of Europe, and Australia (Item 1). I did **not** see an explicit China operating footprint in the extracted sections; **China exposure is unknown** and should be treated as a diligence item (customers, data suppliers, or indirect advertising demand).

**WATCHLIST: ADD** — Data/workflow subscription core + marketplace flywheel, with clear reinvestment levers; diligence integration ROI (Domain/Matterport) and quantify any China-linked revenue/supply dependencies.
