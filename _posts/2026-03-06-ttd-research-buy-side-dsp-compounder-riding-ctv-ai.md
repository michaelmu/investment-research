## The Trade Desk (TTD)

# The Trade Desk (TTD) — buy-side DSP compounder riding CTV + AI automation

**Source anchoring (SEC 10‑K):** Filing **2026-02-27** | Report period ended **2025-12-31** | Accession **0001671933-26-000014** (primary document: `ttd-20251231.htm`).

The Trade Desk is a scaled, independent demand-side platform (DSP) for programmatic advertising. The “compounder” angle is straightforward: a mission‑critical workflow product (campaign execution + measurement) with long-duration client relationships, operating leverage as spend scales, and structural tailwinds (connected TV, retail media, privacy-first measurement, and AI-driven automation).

**Business model / moat:** TTD charges a platform fee generally as a percentage of client platform spend (Item 1). Importantly, it emphasizes ongoing master services agreements (MSAs) rather than episodic insertion orders, and says its **customer retention rate has exceeded 95% for over a decade** (Item 1). That kind of retention suggests the product is embedded and switching costs are non-trivial (process, data integrations, and performance learning loops).

**Scale signals:** TTD states it had **3,843 full-time employees in 21 countries as of Dec 31, 2025**, with workforce split roughly **63% North America / 20% EMEA / 17% APAC** (Item 1). It also discloses that if client relationships were aggregated at the holding-company level, **two holding companies each represented >10% of gross billings in 2025** (and **one >10% in 2024**) (Item 1) — a reminder that agency-holding-company concentration can matter.

**Growth vectors to watch:** Management frames CTV as a “generational shift” away from linear TV and positions its platform + AI tooling (e.g., “Koa” AI co-pilot) to benefit as ad buying becomes more automated and data-driven (Item 1). This is plausible: TV is a large ad category, and programmatic CTV inventory continues to expand.

**Risks / diligence (including China constraint):** This is a global business; Item 1 explicitly calls out investing in several international markets including **China**. The 10‑K text here does not quantify China revenue exposure in Item 1, so treat **China exposure as unknown** and therefore a risk until quantified (e.g., revenue by geography, employees/offices, customer concentration, or regulatory dependencies). Separately, the business relies on privacy‑compliant data and measurement; regulatory shifts that restrict identifiers/cookies can create real execution risk (Item 1).

**10-year price context (trend only, unadjusted):** 10y CAGR ~**26.8%** with a brutal boom/bust: **~‑79.9%** from prior high, last close **$28.46** vs 200‑day SMA **$51.31** (as of 2026-03-06). This looks like a classic “great business, volatile stock” setup — the key is whether fundamentals re-accelerate while unit economics and take-rate discipline persist.

**WATCHLIST:** **ADD** — high-quality, buy-side independent adtech compounder candidate; diligence required on **China exposure (unknown)** + privacy/regulatory headwinds + agency holding-company concentration.
