## Constellation Energy (CEG)

# Constellation Energy (CEG) — clean power bottleneck for AI datacenters

**Category:** enabler (power + reliability)

**AI tailwind thesis (mechanism):** Training/inference scale is turning electricity (and 24/7 firm capacity) into the binding constraint. As hyperscalers sign long-dated contracts to secure *reliable* clean megawatts for datacenters, Constellation’s large nuclear fleet and diversified generation/retail platform is positioned to monetize (1) higher load growth, (2) higher value of firm capacity/clean attributes, and (3) structured, long-term offtake agreements that reduce merchant risk.

**10‑K grounding (Form 10‑K; filingDate 2026‑02‑24; reportDate 2025‑12‑31; accession 0001868275‑26‑000032):**
- **Scale + fleet mix:** After the Calpine acquisition (Jan 2026), Constellation states it has **55 GWs of capacity** across nuclear, natural gas, geothermal, hydro, wind and solar (Item 1, “Our Business”). As of **Dec 31, 2025**, owned generating resources totaled **31,676 MW** and contracted generation **4,798 MW** (Item 1, “Our Operations / Contracted Generation”).
- **Nuclear reliability (the product AI needs):** Constellation reports its nuclear fleet produced **183 TWh** of zero‑emissions electricity in **2025**, and achieved **94.7% nuclear capacity factor** in 2025 (94.6% in 2024) (Item 1, “Nuclear Facilities”).
- **Datacenter-linked contracting example:** The company discloses a **20‑year PPA with Microsoft** to support the restart of Three Mile Island Unit 1 (renamed Crane Clean Energy Center), with ~**835 MW** of emissions‑free capacity once operational, and notes a DOE loan guarantee of up to **$1.0B** to support the restart (Item 1, “Nuclear Facilities”).

**Key KPIs to track:**
- Nuclear capacity factor & refueling outage duration (Item 1).
- Load served (TWh) / customer accounts; renewal rates in retail (Item 1 notes ~2.0M accounts and strong renewal rates).
- Contracting mix: duration/price structure of hyperscaler PPAs; share of EBITDA exposed to merchant power spreads (Item 7 + Notes).
- Progress/timeline on Crane restart + regulatory milestones.

- **Regulatory + nuclear execution:** license extensions, outage risk, safety/cybersecurity, and political risk around nuclear economics (Item 1A).
- **Commodity/market risk:** power/gas price volatility and hedging basis risk (Item 7/7A).
- **Integration risk:** Calpine adds large gas footprint; synergy and operational integration risk.
