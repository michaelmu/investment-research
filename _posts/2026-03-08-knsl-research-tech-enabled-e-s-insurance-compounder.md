## Kinsale Capital Group (KNSL)

# Kinsale Capital (KNSL) — a tech-enabled specialty insurance compounder

**Filing meta (SEC 10-K):** Form 10‑K filed **2026‑02‑20** for fiscal year ended **2025‑12‑31** (accession **0001669162-26-000015**).

Kinsale is an excess & surplus (“E&S”) **property & casualty insurer focused exclusively on the U.S. E&S market**, distributing primarily through independent brokers (Item 1). The basic compounder setup is unusually clean for insurance: disciplined underwriting + structurally low expenses (enabled by workflow/tech) + steady reinvestment of surplus into growth.

Two concrete anchors from the 10‑K highlight why the model can compound:

- **Underwriting profitability is the engine.** Management reports a **2025 combined ratio of 75.9%** (Item 1), implying strong underwriting profit before investment income. They also disclose a **2025 loss & LAE ratio of 55.1%** and **expense ratio of 20.8%** (Item 1). In specialty insurance, this kind of cost structure is a moat because it lets Kinsale price competitively while still earning attractive margins.
- **The business is scaled but not “mature.”** Kinsale’s **2025 gross written premiums were $2.0B (+5.7% YoY)** (Item 1) and they estimate **~1.5% market share** of an E&S market of **~$129.8B** of direct written premiums (Item 1). That combination (large enough to matter, small enough to keep taking share) supports a long runway if underwriting discipline holds.

Distribution concentration is a key diligence item: the **largest broker produced $371.2M (18.8%) of 2025 GWP**, the next **$339.0M (17.1%)**, and the third **$231.2M (11.7%)** (Item 1). If a major broker relationship shifts, growth could slow or costs could rise.

**Price context (10y):** The stock’s ~**37% 10‑year CAGR** (trend context only) is consistent with a high-quality compounding narrative, but shares are currently **~32% below** the 10‑year high and **~13% below the 200‑day SMA**, suggesting sentiment/valuation has de-rated versus peak.

**China exposure:** No explicit China/PRC/HK references surfaced in the extracted 10‑K text. Treat as **low/unknown**; confirm via geographic premium/reinsurance counterparties in the full filing.

**Watchlist action:** **ADD** — E&S specialist with demonstrated underwriting + expense advantage; main diligence items are broker concentration, reserve adequacy through a harder loss cycle, and any hidden international/reinsurance counterparty exposure.
