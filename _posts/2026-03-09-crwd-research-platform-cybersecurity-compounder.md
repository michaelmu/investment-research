## CrowdStrike Holdings, Inc. (CRWD)

# CrowdStrike (CRWD) — platform cybersecurity compounder with a self-reinforcing data flywheel

**SEC 10‑K grounding:** CrowdStrike Holdings, Inc. 10‑K (accession **0001535527-26-000010**), **filed 2026‑03‑05**, **report date 2026‑01‑31**.

CrowdStrike is a cloud-native cybersecurity platform built around a single lightweight endpoint/workload sensor feeding a centralized “Security Cloud.” The core compounding idea is *data gravity*: as more customers run the sensor, CrowdStrike collects more high-fidelity telemetry, which improves detections/automation and makes the platform more valuable, supporting expansion into more modules (endpoint, identity, cloud, next-gen SIEM/log management, etc.). If that feedback loop holds, you get durable share gains plus high incremental economics.

**Two concrete 10‑K facts (and why they matter):**
- **Revenue mix is overwhelmingly subscription:** FY2026 revenue was **$4.812B**, up **22%** YoY, and **subscription revenue was 95% of total revenue** (FY2026 and FY2025). (Item 7, MD&A — “Revenue” table/paragraph.) This is the “compounder substrate”: recurring contracts, module attach, and expansion can stack over time.
- **Contract structure supports visibility:** subscription revenue is recognized ratably over agreements **generally one to three years**, with upfront or installment invoicing leading to meaningful deferred revenue recognition across periods. (Item 7, MD&A — “Subscription Revenue.”) That tends to smooth results and can make sales execution (and renewals) the real swing factor.

**Key risk / thesis stress test:** CrowdStrike explicitly flags that a product/process failure can create lasting commercial damage. The 10‑K highlights the **“July 19, 2024” incident** (a content configuration update that caused Windows system crashes), noting it has had—and is expected to continue to have—adverse effects including customer deferrals/non-renewals, incentives/discounting, and significant costs. (Item 1A, Risk Factors — “The July 19 Incident…”.) For a security brand, reliability and trust are existential.

**China exposure constraint:** I did **not** find explicit “China” mentions in the extracted 10‑K text; international exposure still exists, so treat China-specific revenue/customer exposure as **unknown (risk)** until quantified.
