# FactSet (FDS) — sticky workflow compounder in financial intelligence

**Filing (10-K):** accession **0001628280-25-045769**; filingDate **2025-10-22**; reportDate **2025-08-31**.

## Thesis
FactSet is a “workflow + data” subscription business embedded in the day-to-day operations of investment professionals. The compounding engine is (1) recurring subscription revenue tied to critical workflows (research → portfolio analysis → execution → reporting), (2) high retention, and (3) steady expansion of data/analytics breadth (including AI features) that increases switching costs and wallet share. If the firm keeps converting AI from “feature” into paid modules while defending data cost structure, it can remain a durable mid-to-high single digit grower with strong cash generation.

## 10-K grounded facts (2+; cite Item/section)
- **Scale + recurring model:** As of Aug 31, 2025, FactSet had **~9,000 clients** and **~237,000 professionals**, and revenues are **primarily derived from subscriptions** to multi-asset class data/solutions (Item 1 — Business Overview).
- **Retention signal:** For FY2025, **annual ASV retention was >95%** (and **91%** measured as % of clients) (Item 1 — FactSet Clients and Users).
- **Geographic mix:** FY2025 revenues were **65% Americas / 25% EMEA / 10% Asia Pacific** (Item 1 — Segments).
- **Cash generation:** FY2025 **net cash provided by operating activities was $726.3M** and **free cash flow was $617.5M** (Item 7 — Free Cash Flow).

## Risks / disconfirmers
- **Competitive intensity:** Bloomberg / S&P Market Intelligence / LSEG are named major competitors; any erosion of “must-have” status can pressure pricing/retention (Item 1 — Competitive Landscape).
- **Third-party content costs:** Data supplier concentration exists (two suppliers each >10% of total data costs), and renegotiations can pressure margins (Item 1 — Third-Party Content).
- **Controls issue:** FY2025 includes a **material weakness** in certain IT general controls supporting revenue/AR/deferred revenue processes (Item 8 — Management’s Report on ICFR). Even without misstatements, it’s a quality-of-earnings / operational risk.

## China exposure check
**Some exposure (not zero):** the Asia Pacific segment includes **offices in China and Hong Kong SAR** (Item 1 — Segments) and risk factors cite **China data/security and trade/tariff uncertainty** (Item 1A). I’d treat “ability to operate / move data / retain talent in China” as a non-trivial operational tail risk, though Asia Pacific is only **~10% of revenue**.
