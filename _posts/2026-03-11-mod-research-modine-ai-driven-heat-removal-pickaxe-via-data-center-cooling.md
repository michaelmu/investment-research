## Modine Manufacturing Company (MOD)

#### Modine (MOD) — AI-driven “heat-removal” pickaxe via data center cooling

- **Source 10‑K:** Modine Manufacturing Company Form 10‑K (CIK 0000067347) — **filingDate:** 2025‑05‑21, **reportDate:** 2025‑03‑31, **accession:** 0001558370‑25‑008058.

##### AI tailwind thesis (mechanism)
AI training/inference clusters are pushing data centers toward **higher rack densities** and **higher heat loads**, which increases the complexity (and spend) required to remove heat efficiently. MOD is a second/third‑order AI beneficiary because it sells the thermal infrastructure that must scale with compute: **air, liquid, and hybrid data center cooling products** (incl. liquid cooling distribution / CDUs). Even if specific GPU winners rotate, the “watts in → heat out” requirement persists, and higher performance computing tends to *increase* cooling content per MW deployed.

##### 10-K grounded facts (2+; cite Item/section)
- MOD explicitly connects AI to its product roadmap: it says **AI, machine learning, and other trends are driving increased high‑performance computing**, which increases heat loads and drives demand for **high‑density cooling solutions**; it also notes that in **fiscal 2025 it launched its CDU** for high‑density data center environments. (**Item 1 — Research and Development and Investments in Technology**)
- Growth is showing up in segment results: MOD reports **Climate Solutions segment sales increased $333M (+30%) in fiscal 2025**, “primarily driven by higher sales of **data center cooling** and HVAC&R products,” including **organic sales growth to hyperscale and colocation customers**. (**Item 7 — MD&A / Results of Operations / Climate Solutions**) 
- Customer / concentration frame: MOD says its **ten largest customers accounted for 43%** of consolidated net sales in fiscal 2025; its top customers operate primarily across data center cooling, HVAC&R, and vehicular markets (with additional “global technology customers” under confidentiality agreements). (**Item 1 — Customer Dependence**)

##### KPIs to watch
