## Fortinet (FTNT)

# Fortinet (FTNT) — Security platform + ASIC differentiation compounder

**Source 10-K:** Fortinet, Inc. Form 10-K, **filingDate 2026-02-25**, **reportDate 2025-12-31**, **accession 0001262039-26-000007**.

## Thesis
Fortinet looks like a durable “platform + distribution + economics” compounder in cybersecurity: a broad, integrated product suite (Security Fabric) sold largely through a two-tier channel, with differentiation in performance/cost via proprietary ASICs. If the company continues to win firewall refresh cycles and expands attach rates into SASE/SecOps while maintaining strong operating cash generation, it can compound intrinsic value even without multiple expansion.

## 10-K grounded facts (2+; cite Item/section)
- **Revenue mix is increasingly service/recurring:** for FY2025, **service revenue was $4.58B vs product revenue $2.22B** (Item 8, Consolidated Statements of Income). This supports a “land hardware, expand subscriptions/support” model.
- **Large deferred revenue base:** deferred revenue was **$3.64B current + $3.48B non-current** at 12/31/2025 (Item 8, Consolidated Balance Sheets), indicating meaningful prepaid/contracted obligations that can stabilize near-term revenue.
- **Cash generation is strong (simple FCF proxy):** net cash provided by operating activities was **$2.591B** in 2025 and capex (purchases of property & equipment) was **$364.8M** (Item 8, Consolidated Statements of Cash Flows) → rough **FCF ≈ $2.23B**.

## Risks / disconfirmers
- **Geopolitics + supply chain concentration:** Fortinet notes a large portion of hardware is manufactured in Taiwan (Item 1, Manufacturing and Suppliers) and explicitly flags heightened risk from **China–Taiwan tensions** (Item 1A, Risk Factors).
- **China-related demand restrictions:** risk factors cite that the **Chinese government has instructed domestic companies in certain industries not to use cybersecurity products manufactured by certain companies** (Item 1A; China/trade-policy discussion). That’s a reminder that “secure networking” is politically sensitive.
- **Channel/inventory volatility:** two-tier distribution can create lumpiness (partner ordering, inventory corrections) (Item 1A; demand/forecasting risk discussion).

## China exposure check
- **Operational footprint:** 10-K states it **does not own manufacturing or R&D activities in China** and does not perform source code development/R&D in China (Item 1, Business).
- **Revenue exposure:** **unknown** (no quick geo revenue split surfaced in extracted text). Treat as a risk until quantified.
