## Rollins (ROL)

# Rollins (ROL) — recurring services compounder with steady M&A optionality

*Source:* Rollins, Inc. Form 10-K (accession **0000084839-26-000008**, filingDate **2026-02-12**, reportDate **2025-12-31**).

## Thesis
Rollins is a “boring but powerful” compounder: contracted/recurring pest control routes with high retention, route-density economics, and a long runway for tuck-in acquisitions across local brands. The business is non-discretionary enough to hold up across cycles, while still benefiting from pricing, mix, and operational tech/process improvements.

## 10-K grounded facts (2+; cite Item/section)
- **Scale + recurring visibility:** Rollins serves “more than two million residential and commercial customers” from “more than 800 Company-owned and franchised locations” in “approximately 70 countries,” and highlights the “contracted and recurring nature” of services providing “visibility into a significant portion of our future revenue.” (Business narrative, early Part I section)
- **Strong cash generation:** Management discloses **free cash flow** as operating cash flow minus capex: **Net cash provided by operating activities $678.1M** and **capital expenditures $(28.1)M**, yielding **free cash flow $650.0M** for 2025. (Item 7, MD&A: “Reconciliation of Net Cash Provided by Operating Activities to Free Cash Flow”)
- **Balance sheet not “broken”:** Total stockholders’ equity is **$1.374B** at 12/31/2025 (positive equity), supporting financial flexibility alongside leverage capacity. (Item 8, Consolidated Statements of Financial Position)

## Risks / disconfirmers
- **Valuation risk / “quality trap”:** This is a premium-multiple services compounder; if organic growth slows (pricing, retention, route density) the multiple can compress even if fundamentals remain fine.
- **Execution + acquisition integration:** The compounding model assumes disciplined acquisitions and consistent field execution across many brands/locations.
- **Ownership influence:** A “Significant Shareholder” group beneficially held ~38% of common stock, influencing governance and major transactions. (Risk-type disclosure in Part I text)
