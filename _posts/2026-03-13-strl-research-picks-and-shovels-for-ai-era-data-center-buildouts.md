# Sterling Infrastructure (STRL) — picks-and-shovels for AI-era data center buildouts

## AI tailwind thesis (mechanism)
AI adoption drives multi-year capex cycles in (1) hyperscale + colo data centers and (2) onshoring/expansion of advanced manufacturing (notably semiconductors). STRL is a *2nd/3rd-order* beneficiary because it sells the enabling work around these facilities: large-scale site development and mission-critical electrical/mechanical services. If AI workloads keep rising, customers must add powered shells, substations, switchgear, and campus site work—turning “AI progress” into contracted backlog for specialty contractors.

## 10-K grounded facts (2+; cite Item/section)
- **E‑Infrastructure is explicitly tied to AI-driven buildouts.** In MD&A, management states E‑Infrastructure demand is driven by data center investments and expects growth tied to customer capital plans, noting these investments are driven in part by “increasing adoption and complexity of artificial intelligence applications.” (Item 7 — *Market Outlook and Trends*).
- **Backlog step-change + higher margin mix.** Backlog was **$3.01B** at 12/31/2025 vs **$1.69B** at 12/31/2024, with **book‑to‑burn 1.6x** in 2025; backlog gross margin increased to **17.8%** from **16.7%**. (Item 7 — *Backlog*).
- **Large M&A to deepen mission-critical electrical exposure.** STRL acquired CEC Facilities Group assets for **$562M** (primarily cash + stock) with an earn‑out up to **$80M**; CEC sits in E‑Infrastructure. (Item 7 — *CEC Acquisition*).
