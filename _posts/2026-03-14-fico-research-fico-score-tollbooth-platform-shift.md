# FICO (Fair Isaac) — FICO Score tollbooth + software platform migration

## Thesis
Fair Isaac (FICO) is a rare “tollbooth” compounder: the FICO® Score is deeply embedded in U.S. credit decisioning and distributed at scale through the major credit bureaus, while the Software segment is being repositioned onto FICO® Platform to create a land-and-expand subscription engine. If FICO can keep its Score standard defensible (regulatory + competitive) and successfully migrate/upsell software usage, it can compound cash flows with relatively modest reinvestment needs.

## 10-K grounded facts (2+; cite Item/section)
- **Concentrated distribution via credit bureaus:** revenues generated from agreements with Experian, TransUnion, and Equifax collectively were **51% of total revenues in FY2025** (45% FY2024; 41% FY2023). *(Item 1 — Markets and Customers)*
- **Highly U.S.-centric revenue mix:** **Americas were 87% of FY2025 revenue**, and financial services represented **92% of FY2025 revenue**. *(Item 1 — Markets and Customers; Item 1A also reiterates banking dependence)*
- **Software platform transition is already meaningful:** FICO reports **FICO Platform-based products ARR of $263.6M** as of Sept 30, 2025, **35% of total software ARR**. *(Item 1 — FICO Platform; see also Item 7 discussion of ARR)*
- **Strong cash generation:** net cash provided by operating activities was **$778.8M in FY2025**. *(Item 8 — Consolidated Statements of Cash Flows)*

## Risks / disconfirmers
- **Key partner/customer concentration:** losing or repricing relationships with the three major credit bureaus (and other major customers) could materially hit results. *(Item 1A — reliance on few customers/bureaus)*
- **Regulatory/market risk to the mortgage “standard”:** mortgage-originator choice and alternative score model approvals could reduce FICO Score volumes/pricing power. *(Item 1A — Fannie/Freddie/FHFA risk)*
- **Execution risk in Software shift to cloud/platform:** revenue recognition timing and buyer receptivity to multi-product cloud suites could create volatility or slower growth. *(Item 1A — platform strategy execution)*
